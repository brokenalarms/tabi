Seriously though — RSI is no joke

(the only non-funny section on this page, we promise)

Repetitive Strain Injury affects millions of people who work at computers. Mouse use has been linked to carpal tunnel syndrome, tendonitis, and De Quervain's tenosynovitis. The primary recommendation from ergonomics experts? Reduce unnecessary hand movements.

Tabi Keys eliminates the single most repetitive motion in your workday: the keyboard-to-mouse-and-back dance. Your occupational therapist would approve. Probably. (We're not doctors. But we are keyboard enthusiasts.)

  • Eliminates lateral wrist movement to and from mouse
  • Keeps hands in neutral home-row position
  • Reduces shoulder rotation and elbow deviation
  • Removes fine-motor precision stress (no more pixel-hunting tiny links)
  • Works with split keyboards, vertical keyboards, and any ergonomic setup
  • Compatible with wrist braces (yes, we've checked)
